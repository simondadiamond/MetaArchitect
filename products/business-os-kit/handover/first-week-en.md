# Your first week

*The workspace only becomes yours by using it on real work. Fifteen minutes a day, five
days. That's the whole ask.*

**Day 1 (today, after Simon leaves):** open it, type "do my inbox", send ONE of the
drafts it writes, for real. You've now shipped work with it. That was the hard part.

**Day 2:** paste something messy: a photo of a paper note, a voice memo transcript, a
long customer email. Watch it file things. Check where it put them.

**Day 3:** teach it three things it doesn't know yet ("remember that..."). Tomorrow,
open a fresh conversation and ask about one of them. It will know.

**Day 4:** run your money skill (quote or invoice) on something real and pending.
Compare every number against what you'd have written. Tell it what you'd change.

**Day 5:** type "weekly review". Read the 3-item plan. Do item one.

**The trap to avoid:** treating it like a search engine you visit sometimes. It's a
filing-and-drafting employee. Employees get better because you correct them and tell
them things. Correct it. Tell it things.

**Write down (on this paper) every moment this week where you thought "ugh, it can't do
X".** That list is exactly what Simon builds next session. The annoyances are the roadmap.
