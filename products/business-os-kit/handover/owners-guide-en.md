# Your Business OS: the owner's guide

*One page. Tape it up if it helps. (Print version: no jargon past this line.)*

## What you have

A workspace on your computer that knows your business: your prices, your policies, your
customers, your way of talking. You type to it like you'd text a sharp assistant. It
drafts, files, and remembers. You decide and send.

## The golden rule

**It drafts, you decide.** It never sends, posts, pays, or promises anything on its own.
If it ever claims it did, it's wrong: check your sent mail, trust your eyes, and tell Simon.

## How to talk to it

- Plain words work: "do my inbox", "quote Marie for the deck job", "plan my week".
- Paste anything: emails, a photo of a paper note, a rambling voice memo transcript.
  Messy is fine. That's what the inbox folder is for.
- Tell it facts like you'd tell a new employee: "Marie always pays cash", "we stop
  taking orders December 15". Say "remember that" and it will, permanently.
- Wrong output? Say so, plainly: "too formal", "wrong price, check the file". It reads
  your pricing file, not its imagination, so if a price is wrong, the file is where to look.

## The habit that makes it work

Once a week (Monday coffee is perfect), type: **"weekly review"**. Ten minutes. It reads
everything, tells you where the money is, and gives you a 3-item plan. Skip a week and
nothing breaks; it catches up. Do it most weeks and the whole thing compounds.
