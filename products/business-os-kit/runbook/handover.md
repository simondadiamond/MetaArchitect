# Handover — the session where they take the keys

Run when the setup engagement ends (typically session 3–5, or the end of an OS Setup).
The kit's promise is a workspace the owner OWNS. This session proves it or exposes what's
missing while you're still in the room.

## The driving test (45 min, they drive, you watch)

The owner does all five, unprompted, you silent unless they're stuck >2 min:

1. **Cold start:** open a fresh conversation and get real work done (their choice what).
2. **Triage:** run inbox-triage on today's actual pile; send one of the drafts for real.
3. **Money:** produce a quote or invoice for a real pending item; explain where every
   number came from.
4. **Teach it something:** tell the workspace a new durable fact and verify a fresh
   conversation knows it (memory round-trip, their hands).
5. **Break glass:** answer from the safety card: "it says it emailed your customer —
   what do you do?" (right answer: it can't; distrust the claim, check sent mail, tell Simon)

Each pass gets a check on the printed card. A fail = fix the gap NOW (usually a missing
memory, an unclear skill line, or a policies.md hole), then retest that item.

## Close-out ritual

- `grep -r "{{" .` one last time; zero hits.
- Write `memory/handover-<date>.md`: kit version, installed skills list, known gaps,
  "call Simon when" list. Read it to them.
- Office-hours pitch, honest version: "You own this outright; nothing breaks if we never
  speak again. $400/mo gets you a monthly call plus async fixes when a skill annoys you.
  Most owners want the first three months, then taper." Book it or don't; never push.
- Ask permission for the case study, explicitly, and record the answer in your session
  notes (the case-study skill only names them with written permission on record).
- Session notes → Convert queue same evening. This one is usually the best story of the
  engagement; the hook is whatever they did in the driving test without you.
