## Programming Philosophy — Tactical Barbell Green Protocol (Capacity Phase)

### Phase 1: 12-Week Capacity Phase (Research-Backed)

Simon has a 6-month layoff. Research is clear: this requires the **Green Protocol "Capacity" phase** — NOT a 4–6 week general preconditioning block. The Capacity phase runs **12 weeks** and integrates Operator strength + structured LISS from week 1.

**Why 12 weeks:** A layoff of 12+ weeks constitutes "significant regression" in neural pathways and structural integrity. Previous 1RMs are invalid. The Capacity phase bridges this gap while simultaneously driving fat loss through concurrent training.

#### Load Protocol

- **Training Max (TM):** Start at **50% of pre-detraining estimates**. If squat was ~200 lbs, TM is 100 lbs.
- **Do not let him push heavier because it "feels easy."** Light weight is the goal for the first 4 weeks.
- **Forced progression:** After Week 8, add 5 lbs to upper body TMs and 10 lbs to lower body TMs at the start of each new block — not based on feel.

#### 12-Week Progression Schedule

| Weeks | Operator Intensity | Zone 2 / LISS Duration |
|-------|-------------------|------------------------|
| 1–3   | 70%, 80%, 90% of TM | 30–45 min, 3x/week |
| 4     | Deload (minimal volume, ~50% TM) | Recovery-paced LISS |
| 5–7   | 75%, 85%, 95% of TM | 50–60 min, 3x/week |
| 8     | Deload (minimal volume, ~50% TM) | Recovery-paced LISS |
| 9–11  | Forced progression (+5/10 lbs to TM) | 60+ min, 3x/week |
| 12    | Taper / Assessment | Taper |

#### Phase 2 (after Week 12)

Transition to full Operator/Black or Operator/Green continuation. Increase TM by 5 lbs (upper) / 10 lbs (lower) per block. Keep the same 3-day strength structure.

### Zone 2 Protocol

**Target HR:** Use the Maffetone 180 Formula: `180 − age − 5 (post-layoff adjustment)`. At age 40 that's `180 − 40 − 5 = 135 bpm max`; training zone **125–135 bpm**. Recompute from Simon's current age (derive from DOB) — don't carry the 40 forward.

**Without a HR monitor — use the Talk Test:** He must be able to hold a full conversation without gasping. If he can't speak in complete sentences, he's gone anaerobic. Nasal breathing only is another reliable indicator.

**What counts as LISS (cardiac adaptation):**
Sessions must be **uninterrupted** and **30+ minutes** to trigger eccentric cardiac hypertrophy and mitochondrial density gains.

- ✅ **45-min morning scrum walk on treadmill** = Zone 2 session IF HR is in the zone. At ~220 lbs detrained, roughly 3.0–3.5 mph at 0% incline or 1–2% incline should hit the zone. Verify with the talk test.
- ✅ **Rucking (30 lb plate, outdoor)** = Zone 2 session — but the rucksack increases cardiac load, so he'll need to **slow the pace** vs bodyweight walking to stay under the HR ceiling.
- ✅ **Rucking on the under-desk treadmill during scrums** = Zone 2 if pace is dialed back to keep HR in the zone.
- ❌ **Slow under-desk walking (1–2 mph, no load)** = NEAT, not Zone 2. Still valuable — accumulated NEAT is a major driver of fat loss. Just doesn't remodel the heart.

**NEAT vs Zone 2:**
- **Zone 2**: Uninterrupted 30+ min in the target HR zone → triggers cardiac remodeling, mitochondrial density, lowers resting HR over time.
- **NEAT**: All other movement → burns calories, prevents metabolic slowdown, contributes directly to the fat loss goal. Both matter. Don't conflate them.

**Summary:** 3 Zone 2 sessions/week. Morning scrum (with pace or rucksack to hit HR zone) covers one. Outdoor rucking covers another. A third dedicated session covers the third.

### Strength Cluster (Operator 3-Day)

**Lift selection:**
- **Primary cluster:** Back Squat, Bench Press, Weighted Pull-up
- **Deadlift:** Once per week (Friday only) to manage systemic fatigue. 1–3 sets.
- **No more than 3 sets per lift** during Phase 1 to stay under 30 minutes and manage CNS load.
- **Rest intervals:** Strict 2 minutes between sets.

**Finisher — Loaded Carry (every strength day, 2–3 min):**
Farmer's walk or goblet carry with 50 lb KB. Walk back and forth in the available space until 2 minutes is up. Targets grip, postural stability under load, and midline bracing. Borrowed from Dan John's methodology.

**Substitution rules:**
- If shoulder issues → replace Bench with Overhead Press
- If no pull-up progression available → replace with KB Row (50 lb)

### Kettlebell Conditioning

Kettlebells are the primary GPP/conditioning tool. Barbell is for strength. These are separate.

**Movement hierarchy:**
1. **KB Swings (50 lb)** — Gold standard for posterior chain conditioning.
2. **Goblet Squats (35 lb)** — Structural priming, squat mechanics, ligament prep.
3. **Single-Arm KB Row (50 lb)** — Mid-back endurance, unilateral stability.
4. **KB Floor Press or push-ups** — Horizontal push endurance.
5. **Turkish Get-Ups (35 lb)** — Core stability and shoulder health; recovery/GPP days.

**HIC day (Tuesday):** 10 sets × 10–20 swings with 50 lb bell.

**GPP day (Thursday) — 20-minute circuit:**
- 3 rounds: 60 sec per exercise, 30–60 sec rest between
- Swings (50 lb) → Goblet Squats (35 lb) → KB Row (50 lb) → KB Floor Press or push-ups
- If form breaks down on 50 lb → drop to 35 lb immediately. Form > load in Phase 1.

## Daily Mobility Protocol (Non-Negotiable)

Every day — before the main session or as a standalone 10-minute habit. **Active methods only** — no passive static holds.

Run as one fluid circuit, ~8–10 minutes total:

1. **PNF Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch** — 2 × 1 min/side.
   - Half-kneel position. Drive the trailing knee into the floor (10 sec isometric contraction). Relax. Squeeze the glute harder and sink deeper. Repeat.
   - Cue: *"Tuck your tailbone under and squeeze the glute like you're crushing a walnut."*

2. **ATG Split Squat** — 2 × 10/side (bodyweight to start; add load as mobility improves).
   - Long step forward. Sink hips straight down, front knee tracking over toes, back glute tight.
   - Cue: *"Torso upright, hips drop straight down — not forward."*

3. **Powell Raises + Trap 3 Raises** — superset, 1 × 15 each side (no rest between).
   - **Powell Raise:** Lie on your side. Raise the top arm straight toward the ceiling, top two knuckles in line with your ear.
   - **Trap 3 Raise:** Bent-over 45°. Pull the shoulder blade toward the spine, then raise the arm at 45° away from the head until your ear is visible past your bicep.
   - Cue: *"Retraction first, then raise — not a shrug."*

4. **Bird Dogs** — 2 × 10/side.
   - Opposite arm and leg extended. Push heel back, hand forward. Neutral spine.
   - Cue: *"Push your heel to the wall behind you. Don't let your lower back sag."*

5. **Cat-Cow → Thoracic Rotation** — 1 min/side.
   - Cue: *"Follow your elbow with your eyes. The rotation starts at your ribs, not your shoulder."*

**Progression note:** ATG split squats start as a stretch. Over Phase 1 (12 weeks), gradually increase depth. By Phase 2, load with a goblet hold or add a heel elevation.
