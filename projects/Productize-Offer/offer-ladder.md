## 1b. Pricing Strategy for a Zero-Proof Starter (locked)

The research is consistent: a starter with no social proof should **never buy trust with a lower price — buy it with lower risk.** Cheap pricing signals risk to a B2B buyer whose own reputation is on the line; risk reversal signals confidence. Strategy, in order of leverage:

1. **Never discount pre-emptively.** Price at market (§1 bands). The founding rate is not a discount — it is payment in proof assets (testimonial, case study, referrals), consideration named in the SOW. When a prospect pushes back on price, trade scope, not price.
2. **De-risk the entry, don't de-price it.** The Diagnostic is productized: fixed scope, fixed price, fixed timeline, named deliverables. Clarity substitutes for credentials — a tightly specified $2,500 engagement is easier to buy from a stranger than a vague $1,000 one.
3. **Risk reversal on the Diagnostic (better-than-risk-free — reaffirms the 2026-05-09 locked frame).** If the findings memo isn't worth the fee, full refund on request within 14 days of the readout — and the client keeps every deliverable. B2B guarantees convert because the buyer risks reputation, not just money; visible guarantees lift conversion ~20-26% in published cases, and the refund exposure on a 2–3 day engagement is trivial against a $6,500 audit pipeline. Guarantee applies to the Diagnostic only — audits and retainers are de-risked by the ladder itself (each tier is proof for the next).
4. **Results in advance.** Free proof surfaces do the trust work: /score quiz, teardowns, STATE artifacts, the 15-minute discovery call. Expertise proof (public work) substitutes for client proof (testimonials) until the founding cohort produces the real thing.
5. **Price floor $2,000.** Below that, an "audit" reads as a disguised sales call and attracts the wrong buyer. (This is what kills the live $750 SKU.)
6. **Raise by mechanism, not mood.** Full rates activate via the slot mechanics — founding closes when slots fill or backstop dates pass, not when Simon feels ready.

## 3. Amendments to the Founding Doc

**Amendment 1 — Retainer day-blocks (capacity conflict).** The doc's 2-day/week retainer must be delivered for 3+ consecutive months *while Simon still holds a full-time W-2* — that's ~16 delivery hours/week on top of 40, for a quarter, and it's the single most likely failure point of the whole plan. Fix: sell the retainer in day-blocks with the 1-day/week on-ramp ($5,500/mo founding). Lower client commitment barrier, survivable delivery load, and a built-in expansion motion (1→2 days). Capacity rule: max 2 committed days/week total across all retainers while the W-2 exists.

**Amendment 2 — No mid-term price step.** The doc auto-steps $9k→$12k at month 4 — a +33% increase exactly when the quit trigger needs the client to hold months 1–3. That's a churn cliff placed on the trigger window. Fix: founding rate holds for the initial 6-month term; steps to full rate at renewal with 60 days' written notice. Founding consideration becomes $3k/mo × 6 = $18k — larger, cleaner, no cliff.

**Amendment 3 — Tiered exchange terms.** The full §5 exchange (non-blockable case study, logo rights, retroactive invoicing) on a $2,500 SKU is disproportionate friction for a first-ever client, and the retroactive-invoice clause is practically unenforceable at a $1,000 delta. Fix: **Diagnostic founding** = approval-required testimonial + 30-min recorded debrief + 2 warm intros if satisfied. **Audit & Retainer founding** = full §5 exchange, lawyer-reviewed SOW. (This also reconciles the goals-row variant "LinkedIn recommendation + case study rights + 2 warm referrals" — that's now the diagnostic tier.)

**Amendment 4 — Funnel requires outbound (see §4).** The doc's three-path table is inbound-shaped; at 254 followers with zero clients, inbound cannot feed it in time.

**Amendment 5 — CAD invoicing for Canadian-domiciled clients (added 2026-07-03).** The 2026-05-09 "bill in USD" rule predates the ladder and adds FX friction for the primary ICP (Canadian regulated orgs — procurement prefers CAD). Fix: fixed CAD equivalents, rounded, repriced quarterly, never mid-engagement: Diagnostic **$3,500 / $4,800 CAD** · Audit **$9,000 / $13,000 CAD** · Retainer **$7,500 (1 d/wk) / $12,500 (2 d/wk) founding, $9,500 / $16,500 full CAD/mo**. USD list prices remain canonical and are what the site displays; CAD applies at invoice for Canadian entities. Rate integrity preserved — these are conversions, not discounts.

**Amendment 6 — Team Training tier inserted (added 2026-07-03, Simon-approved).** The founding doc and the original three-tier ladder had no capability-transfer offer, which lost every audit client who couldn't budget a monthly retainer and gave kit-inbound engineering managers (§9) nothing to buy below $5,500/mo. Fix: **"Agentic Development for Teams"** workshops as tier 3 (§2) — half-day $3,000 founding / $4,500 full, full-day $4,500 founding / $6,500 full, market-checked 2026-07-03 (sources and the half-day adjustment rationale in §2 notes). Remote-first, on-site travel billed to client, EN/FR (francophone Quebec delivery is a near-empty niche). Fixed-scope and fixed-price only — never a retainer-in-disguise (brief's time-risk rule). **Explicitly NOT the parked Phase 4 workshop** from the roadmap: that was an audience-funnel practitioner workshop gated on ~1K followers; this is a B2B engagement sold 1:1 through the ladder and via kit inbound — it needs one buyer, not an audience, so the roadmap's sequencing rule is not violated.

## 4. Is the Oct 2026 retainer target credible?

**On inbound alone: no.** The diagnostic funnel needs ~6 qualified discovery calls to produce 5 founding diagnostics → ~2 audits → 1 retainer. Current inbound reach (254 followers, commenting cadence not started, 1 blog post, /score quiz live but unfed) plausibly produces 1–2 qualified calls by October. The math doesn't close.

**With outbound started in July: Oct 2026 is the stretch case, Dec 2026–Feb 2027 is the honest estimate.** Outbound = the 20–30 named-prospect list (Quebec/Canadian orgs, production GenAI, Law 25/OSFI exposure, per founding doc §2 gates) + warm-network reactivation. A warm-path hit can land a diagnostic in weeks and a retainer by October; cold-start ICP outreach realistically adds one quarter. Every week outbound doesn't start slips this window a week — it is the schedule-critical path, not content.

**DECISION-1 (Simon, required to fully lock):** the quit trigger. Options:
- **(a) Keep as-is** — $8k MRR × 3 *delivered* months. Safest; means one brutal quarter of moonlighting delivery (survivable at the 1-day on-ramp + audits, hard at 2 days/wk).
- **(b) Contracted trigger** — quit when MRR ≥ $8k on a signed ≥6-month term AND month 1 is delivered + paid. Faster exit, more risk on a single client's signature.
- COO recommendation: **(a) with the day-block on-ramp** — the on-ramp exists precisely to make the delivered-months rule survivable. Revisit (b) only if a 2-day retainer signs first.
