## How to work this list (COO operating rules)

1. **Warm-path pass first (Simon, ~20 min, once).** Walk the table's empty "Warm path?" column: 1st/2nd-degree LinkedIn connections, ex-colleagues, Quebec tech community overlap. Any row with a warm path jumps a full priority letter. Warm beats ranked — a warm C+ outranks a cold A.
2. **Cadence: 5 first-touches per week, A-rows first.** First touch is never a pitch — it's a specific, substantive comment or DM about *their* system (the evidence URL gives you the opener; e.g., nesto's "fully auditable, traceable" claim, MEDFAR's CoeurWay scribe rollout). Ask for the 15-minute fit check only on the second touch. This runs on the same daily block as the superstar commenting cadence — same muscle, different targets.
3. **Every call runs the script.** No founding rate quoted before the exchange paragraph. Log every call outcome (prospect / quoted / not-now + gates failed) — in `founding_clients` once the table exists, in a dated section at the bottom of this file until then.
4. **French first for FR-flagged rows** (MEDFAR, Croesus, Beneva, Desjardins, NBC, Dialogue, Coveo). The bilingual founding perk is the differentiator — lead with it.
5. **E-23 is the 2026 opener for every insurer/FI row**: "OSFI E-23 lands May 2027 and your GenAI workflows are now model inventory — who's writing your validation story?" Law 25 stacks on top for Quebec rows.
6. **Refresh rule:** any row whose evidence is >12 months old (Beneva) gets a 10-minute re-check before outreach. Re-check Plusgrade (excluded on evidence, not fit) in January 2027.
7. **Falsification checkpoint (from offer-ladder.md §8):** if zero discovery calls booked by **2026-08-15**, the outbound volume is the problem — double weekly touches before touching prices.

## Summary

**30 named orgs, all with verified evidence URLs.** 15 are Quebec-based or Quebec-anchored (50%), 15 across Ontario, Alberta, BC, and Atlantic Canada. Evidence quality: 18 orgs have **strong** evidence (a named GenAI/LLM product or workflow live for real users, from a primary source — press release, engineering blog, case study, or credible trade press); 9 have **medium** evidence (production AI confirmed but GenAI depth or recency less certain, or vendor-deployed); 3 are **weaker but strategic** (kept for coverage of a requested segment, flagged C). No rows are padded — five candidates that failed the filter are documented in the exclusions section.

**Strongest 5 leads:**

1. **MEDFAR (Montreal)** — Quebec's dominant EMR (MYLE, ~half of Quebec clinics) bought AI-scribe company CoeurWay and pushed generative note-writing to 2,000+ clinicians. PHI + Law 25 + French-first + mid-size (~300 staff) + AI now core product strategy. Almost the platonic founding client.
2. **nesto (Montreal)** — $1.47B digital mortgage lender publicly promising underwriting via "Maestro AI" that is "fully auditable, traceable and compliant." They are marketing Simon's exact vocabulary; someone inside has to make it true. Financial PII, mid-size, Quebec, FR/EN.
3. **AlayaCare (Montreal)** — Layla AI assistant on Amazon Bedrock, launched in Canada June 2025, live over home-care PHI. Mid-size (~600), HIPAA/PIPEDA claims already public — an audit maps directly onto their compliance story.
4. **Blue J (Toronto)** — RAG tax-research product with published accuracy benchmarking (91.9%) and a "disagree button" feedback loop. A company that already measures LLM accuracy publicly is pre-sold on evals and reliability. ~200 staff, sensitive tax/financial data.
5. **League (Toronto)** — "Agent Teams" serving 63M contracted users and 100M+ AI recommendations over claims/clinical data. The gap between that scale and any sane eval/observability story is the pitch. Committee risk only moderate (~500 staff).

Honourable mentions: **Jane App** (AI Scribe over PHI, PIPEDA), **Spellbook** (4,000 legal teams on LLM contract review), **Verafin** (agentic AML at 650+ financial institutions).

**Cross-cutting hook:** OSFI's final Guideline E-23 (model risk management, explicitly covering AI/ML) takes effect **May 1, 2027** and now covers *all* FRFIs including insurers ([OSFI](https://www.osfi-bsif.gc.ca/en/guidance/guidance-library/guideline-e-23-model-risk-management-2027), [Blakes analysis](https://www.blakes.com/insights/osfi-releases-final-guideline-e-23-for-model-risk-management-and-ai-use-by-frfis/)). Every bank, insurer, and trust company row below inherits a 2026 "get your model inventory and validation story ready" urgency. Quebec rows additionally inherit Law 25's automated-decision transparency duties.

## Deliberately Excluded (teaches the filter)

| Org | Why excluded |
|-----|--------------|
| **RBC / TD / BMO / CIBC / Scotiabank** | Production GenAI evidence is abundant, but each has hundreds of internal MLOps/model-risk staff, Borealis-style AI labs, and procurement that a solo consultant cannot survive. Even the "team lead wedge" is better spent on NBC/Desjardins where the FR angle differentiates. |
| **Shopify** | World-class production LLM usage, but no regulated-data wedge (merchant commerce data), a build-everything engineering culture, and enterprise scale. Fails gates 2 and 3. |
| **Cohere** | Runs LLMs in production by definition — but they are a model vendor. Selling reliability consulting to a foundation-model company is selling sand at the beach, and any engagement creates channel conflict with Simon's own positioning. |
| **Plusgrade (Montreal)** | Attractive size and city, but the only public evidence found is ML job postings ([Lever posting](https://jobs.lever.co/plusgrade/1fe71c80-2cf5-4f98-bfc2-433ec3f5c77a)) — hiring intent, not a verified production GenAI workflow, and no regulated-data angle (ancillary travel revenue). Fails gate 1 evidence bar. Re-check in 6 months. |
| **Questrade / Trulioo / Flinks** | All three surfaced in research but were cut on the same principle: Questrade's public GenAI story is Gemini-in-Workspace employee productivity ([Google blog](https://workspace.google.com/blog/customer-stories/questrade-financial-group-transforms-productivity-gemini-google-workspace)) — internal copilot rollout is not a production LLM workflow with reliability stakes; Trulioo's and Flinks' production AI is classic CV/ML classification, not GenAI/LLM state problems. "Uses AI" ≠ "runs an LLM workflow that can fail in production." |
