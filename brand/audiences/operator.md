## Label

**The Expertise Operator** — owner-operator of a service/expertise business, solo up to ~5 people: consultants, coaches, trainers/facilitators, fractional executives, boutique agency principals, solo professionals. Archetype: client #0 (Simon's sister).

**Qualifying refinement (stress test 2026-07-20): the delegation-proven operator.** The strongest buyers already pay humans for ops relief — a VA, OBM, or bookkeeper at $500–1,500/mo. They have demonstrated willingness to spend on operations; the workspace displaces spend they already make. Discovery-call qualifier: "Do you already pay for help with this?"

## The defining sentence

> "I'm paying for Claude and I know it should be saving me hours, but every chat starts from zero — I re-explain my business, re-paste the same three documents, and the draft still doesn't sound like me. I don't need another AI tool. I need my business set up so the AI I already pay for actually knows it."

## Firmographics

- Owner-operator; the buyer IS the person doing the work (single machine, single Claude account). "Solo to ~5" is shorthand for where that's usually true, NOT a headcount cutoff — see the boundary rule under Out of scope
- Revenue roughly $100K–$1M; bills $100–250/hr or equivalent
- Already pays for Claude (or ChatGPT) and uses ~10% of it
- Recurring knowledge work: invoices, proposals, client follow-ups, onboarding, workshop emails, content in their own voice

## 5 core frustrations

1. **Groundhog-day context** — every session starts from zero; re-explaining the business for the hundredth time.
2. **Generic output** — drafts read like a press release from a company they've never met; editing takes longer than writing would have.
3. **Owner-as-bottleneck** — invoices, proposals, follow-ups, onboarding all wait on the owner's evenings.
4. **Paying for 10%** — real money going to AI subscriptions that don't compound; quiet guilt about underuse.
5. **No memory** — decisions get re-litigated because nothing persists; "where did we land on that?"

## Language that lands

"I re-explain my business every single time" | "It sounds like AI wrote it" | "I'm the bottleneck" | "Admin eats my evenings" | "I'm using maybe 10% of what I pay for" | "Where did we land on that?"

**Never use tool language with this audience.** No "agents", "context windows", "orchestration", "MCP", "state management" on operator-facing surfaces. Business pain in business words: invoices, follow-ups, evenings, sounding like yourself. (Stress-test finding: the AI-curious crowd that likes tool language is the DIY crowd that doesn't buy.)

## Voice test — the Busy Owner Test

Would an owner who spent last Sunday night doing invoices read this and think "that's my Tuesday"?

## Positioning vs. Claude for Small Business (required on every sales surface)

Anthropic launched **Claude for Small Business** on 2026-05-13: 15 ready-to-run workflows + 7 connectors (QuickBooks, PayPal, HubSpot, Canva, Docusign, Google Workspace, M365), a toggle inside Claude Cowork. The buyer's free alternative is now an official product, and "isn't Claude doing this for free?" will come up on discovery calls.

The wedge — never argue against it, build on top of it:
- Their words: "ready-to-run", pre-built. Ours: **built around work you already do.** A toggle can't hold your voice, your rates, your onboarding checklist, or what you decided about fall pricing.
- The free tier proves the demand and teaches the category. Position the OS Setup as what comes after the toggle disappoints: generic workflows for generic businesses vs. a workspace that knows YOURS.
- Concrete line for copy: "Claude now ships ready-made workflows for a generic small business. You don't run a generic small business."
