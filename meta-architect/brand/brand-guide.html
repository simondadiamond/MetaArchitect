06 — Voice & Tone

Writing Rules

Practitioner voice, not theory voice. Every sentence passes the test: would someone who's been burned by a production LLM failure read this and think "yes, exactly"?

Write This Not This
"Your agent isn't failing because of the model. Here's what's actually breaking." "I'm excited to share some thoughts on AI reliability challenges."
"Always assume the LLM will fuck up. Design for it." "It's important to implement robust testing in your AI systems."
"The backtick thing happened to me at 11pm, client demo the next morning." "As a practitioner, I've seen many cases where JSON parsing can fail."
"Can we log why the agent did this? Law 25 says you need to answer that." "Organizations should consider regulatory compliance in their AI journey."
"It's not about the model — it's about the plumbing around it." "Game-changing LLM architectures for the future of enterprise AI."
01
Specific over general. Name the tool, the error, the exact failure mode. "The LLM wrapped its JSON in markdown backticks" beats "parsing issues can occur."
02
Practitioner voice, not expert voice. You earned the right to teach through failure, not credentials. The scar is the credential.
03
Make the audience feel seen first. They've hit this wall. They've been blamed the model. Start there before offering the fix.
04
No hype language. Cut: "game-changing," "revolutionary," "the future of AI," "I'm excited to share," "transformational."
05
Dry wit is allowed. "Always assume the LLM will fuck up" is on-brand. Careful precision and sharp humor coexist here.
06
French is natural, not forced. Bilingual brain. Quebec context appears organically — never performatively.

01
Zero border-radius everywhere. No rounding, ever. Sharp edges are the architectural signature of the brand.
02
1px borders in #333333. Borders separate surfaces; they never decorate. One weight, one color throughout.
03
Max-width 1100px, centered. Wide enough for grids, narrow enough to stay readable. Horizontal padding 32px.

Grid gaps via 1px background trick. Set gap:1px on grid, background:var(--border). Elements fill with surface color. Clean dividers without extra markup.

08 — Design Rules

Non-Negotiables

These are the load-bearing constraints of the visual system. The equivalent of the STATE framework, applied to design.

01
Always dark mode. No light mode variant. Pure black backgrounds. This is not a toggle — it's a statement.
02
Orange is the primary action color. Buttons, CTAs, highlights, brand accents. One action color.
03
Amber for links only. #C97A1A on interactive text elements. Distinct from orange, in the same family. Never blue.
04
Red is reserved for errors. Never decorative. Never as a brand color. Destructive and error states only.
05
High contrast text. Primary text on background = 14:1+ ratio (WCAG AAA). Readability is non-negotiable.
06
Zero border-radius everywhere. Sharp edges, no rounding. Architectural precision, not friendly softness.
07
No teal, no green, no blue. Two accent families: orange (action) and amber (links). That's the complete palette.
08
Mono font for all labels and navigation. Roboto Mono with letter-spacing 0.1–0.2em on uppercase labels. Technical precision as aesthetic.
